Setting Up the Battlefield

Before you can start fighting for control, you need to prepare the game board:

  1. Set the Target to 2.

  2. Place the Operation Marker on the Target Zone’s Flag.

  3. Add 2 Tanks to each Player’s Supply.

  4. Give each player 4 Squads and 3 Resources.

  5. Flip a coin to determine the first player.

  6. Starting with the second player, each player chooses a side, then places all of their Squads on that side’s Spawn Points.

  7. Deal 4 Action Cards to each player. Each player keeps one and hands the rest to the other. Repeat until each player has picked 2 cards. Those form their hands. The rest of the cards go back into the Action Card deck and it is shuffled.

  8. The first player can now take their turn!

At the start of the game, only the bottom Spawn Points are available.

Units

Each Unit is represented by a six-sided die. The value shown on top of them represents their current strength.

Unit Types
Squad

The most basic of Units, a squad of armed soldiers.

Stats

  • Strength: 1 to 6

  • Range: 2

Tank

Equipped with highly advanced tech, the Tank is a strong combat unit.

Stats

  • Strength: 2 to 6

  • Range: 3

Machinery

If at any moment this Unit’s strength reaches 2, it is destroyed.
Permanently remove it from the game and replace it with a Squad with 1 strength.
Any remaining losses are discarded.

Fullmetal

If the defender is defeated, skip the Counter Attack phase.

Motorized

No more than once, after moving during a March, it may do 1 free movement.

Player Turns

During their turn, a player can take up to 2 actions. The same action may be taken more than once.

Player Actions
March

Take up to 3 movements with any of your Units.

Attack

Choose a Unit to attack with (the "attacker") and an enemy target Unit that’s within range (the "defender").
The player that’s attacking does as follows, in order:

  1. The defender takes losses equal to the attackers strength + 1, minus their strength. (On a tie, the defender takes 1 loss.)

  2. The defender does a Counter Attack.

  3. Once an involved Unit's strength reaches 0, they are defeated.

  4. If the attacker was defeated, the opponent gains 1 VP. Move the Unit to any Spawn Point that’s not adjacent to the Target Zone, they regain 2 strength.

  5. If the defender was defeated:

    1. Move the defender to any Spawn Point that’s not adjacent to the Target Zone, they regain 2 strength.

    2. Choose one of the following:

      1. Eliminate: Gain 2 VP.

      2. Research: Gain 1 VP and 1 Resource.

  6. Otherwise, if the defender is in a Village, Tower or Ballista, move it to any adjacent space.

Adjacent spaces are orthogonal: Up, down, left and right.
Diagonal spaces are non-adjacent.
Counter Attack

When a Unit is attacked, it can do a counter-attack, even if it isn’t within attack range.
When this happens, the attacker takes 1 loss.

You cannot counter a counter-attack.
Action Cards

At the start of their turn, a player may play a single Action Card from their hand, this does not count as an action.
The player can also ignore the card’s effect to gain 1 Resource until the end of their turn.
Once it’s been played, the card goes to the Action Card discard pile.

Emergency Upgrade

Pay 1 Resource.
Upgrade one of your Units into a 4 strength Tank.
(You can do this even if you have no Tanks on your supply.)

Forward!

Do a March action, but you cannot move the same Unit more than once.
( Tanks do not get any free movement).

Sabotage

Pay 1 Resource.
Make a single movement with an enemy Unit.

- OR -

Pay 2 Resources.
Make up to 3 movements with a single enemy Unit.

Exhaustion

During the opponent’s next turn, they make -1 movement on their March action(s).

Fuel Shortage

Pay 1 Resource.
During their next turn, the opponent cannot move any Tanks with their March action.

Bullseye

On your next Attack action during this turn, the defender takes +1 loss.

Supply and Demand

During your opponent’s next turn, Upgrading costs +1 Resource.

Bribe

Pay 1 Resource.
The Contest Marker advances or goes back one space.

Navigation Expert

On your March action(s) during this turn, ignore extra movement costs.

Take Cover!

Pay 1 Resource.
During the opponent’s next turn, your defending Units have +1 strength.
(A Unit can never have more than 6 strength.)

The Battlefield

Units move through the Battlefield to fight, gain resources and contest flags.

Terrain Types
Ground

The default terrain type.
Has no special effects.

Spawn Point

Has no special effects.

Water

Costs an additional movement to leave.
While standing in Water, a Unit cannot counter-attack and has -1 strength when defending.
Tanks cannot move into Water.

Bridge

To enter a Bridge costs an additional movement for Tanks.
(Their free movement can be used for that.)

Forest

While standing in a Forest, a Unit cannot suffer a counter-attack and has +1 strength when defending.
Tanks cannot move into a Forest.

Hill

Costs an additional movement to enter.
While standing a Hill, a Unit has +2 range (unless it is a Tank) and cannot suffer a counter-attack.

Flag

Entering a Flag space in the Target Zone starts a Contest.

Contested

No movement can be done on a Contested space.

Village

At the end of your turn, a Unit that’s standing in a Village may take one of the following actions:

Rest

The Unit regains 2 strength, up to a maximum of 4.

Hire

Pay up to 2 Resources to hire mercenaries.
The Unit gains +1 strength per Resource spent, up to a maximum of 6.

Prep

Draw an Action Card, then discard your hand down to 2.
Then, if the Action Card deck is empty, take the discard pile and shuffle it to make a new one.

Tower

While standing in a Tower, a Unit has +1 range (unless it is a Tank).
At the end of your turn, a Unit that’s standing in a Tower may take one of the following actions:

Upgrade

Pay 2 Resources to upgrade any of your Squads into a Tank.
The strength of the Tank is equal to the old Squad's strength + 2.
The old Squad goes into the player’s supply if possible.
(A Unit that’s standing in a Village cannot be upgraded.)

Relocate

Make up to 2 movements with a single Unit.
( Tanks don’t get a free movement.)

Ballista

At the start of your turn, a Unit that’s standing in a Ballista may take the following action:

Shoot

Pay 1 Resource to attack an enemy Unit within 5 spaces of the Ballista.
The enemy Unit takes 3 losses and cannot counter-attack.

Windmill

A Unit cannot finish their movement on a Windmill if there are any Resources on it.
At the end of your turn, if there is at least one Unit adjacent to a Windmill, take one Resource from it.

You cannot Upgrade if you don’t have any Tanks in your supply.
To "enter" or "leave" a space, means to go from one terrain type to another.
Moving from one Water tile to another, for example, costs no additional movement.
Terrain actions are always free.
They do not count towards the 2 actions per turn.

Contests

Whenever a Unit enters a Flag space in the Target Zone, a Contest is started:

  1. The player who started the Contest gains 1 VP.

  2. Take the Contest Marker and place it on the green spot of the Contest Tracker.

  3. At the end of every second turn, the Contest Marker advances 1 space on the Contest Tracker.

Once the Contest Marker reaches the red spot of the Contest Tracker, the Contest is finished:

  1. The player who controls the most Units inside the Target Zone gains 3 VPs.

  2. Place a Contested cube on the Target Zone’s Flag.

    1. If there’s a Unit standing in the Flag space, its owner moves it to an adjacent space.

  3. Roll the Target die to determine the new Target Zone.

  4. Place the Contest Marker on the new Target Zone’s Flag.

A zone containing a Contested cube cannot be a Target Zone.
A "zone" is a 5x5 area represented by a striped outline around a Flag space.

Ending a Game

The game ends once:

  1. All six Flags have been contested.

  2. A player has reached 10 VPs.

The player with the most VPs is the winner!

In a tie, consider the following tie breakers, in order:

  1. Amount of Resources

  2. Total Unit strength
